The Human Mind 2
Main parts of the brain: Cerebrum: The largest part, responsible for thinking, memory, and decision-making Cerebellum: Controls balance and coordination Brainstem: Manages basic life functions like breathing and heartbeat The brain sends and receives signals through nerves, allowing the body to react quickly.
How do dreams work? While you’re asleep, especially during REM sleep (Rapid Eye Movement), your brain is almost as active as when you’re awake. Some parts, like the emotional center (amygdala), become even more active, which is why dreams can feel so intense or dramatic. Dreams are like your brain mixing memories, thoughts, and imagination together. It pulls random pieces from your day (or even years ago) and creates a story—even if it makes zero sense. You don’t just have one dream per night. You can have 4–6 dreams every night, but you forget most of them within minutes of waking up. Sometimes people realize they’re dreaming while still in the dream. This is called lucid dreaming, and with practice, some people can even control what happens.
